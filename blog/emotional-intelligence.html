How is EQ different from IQ?

Emotional Quotient (EQ) is one method that is used to measure Emotional Intelligence. Its name appears to be a counter to the popular Intelligence Quotient (IQ). IQ is designed to be a score of human intelligence, or more specifically, cognitive intelligence.

Overall
IQ focuses on cognitive processes and is often seen as a predictor of academic and technical performance.
EQ focuses on emotional and social skills, which are crucial for interpersonal relationships, leadership, and overall life satisfaction.

Abilities
IQ attempts to measure logical reasoning, mathematical skills, spatial ability, language comprehension, and problem-solving skills.
EQ attempts to assess one‚Äôs self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills.

Testing
IQ scores are derived from standardised tests, and scores are intended to be a comparison to the general human population.
EQ is often assessed through self-report questionnaires, behavioural assessments, and peer reviews.

Development
IQ scores are supposedly relatively stable throughout one‚Äôs adult life; while one can improve certain cognitive tasks, the overall innate cognitive abilities don‚Äôt change.
EQ abilities are said to be able to be improved over time with practice.
